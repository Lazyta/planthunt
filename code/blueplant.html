Sarracenia purpurea

Like other species of Sarracenia, S. purpurea obtains most of its nutrients through prey capture.
Evidence by growers often shows that pitchers quickly fill up with prey during the warm summer months.
Prey fall into the pitcher and drown in the rainwater that collects in the base of each leaf. Prey items such as flies, ants, spiders, and even moths.
